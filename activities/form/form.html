The form tag has an "action" attribute. What does it do?

The action attribute is the same as clicking a link. It directs you to another page.

The form tag has a "method" attribute. What does it do?

The method attribute determines how the information submitted by the user in the form will be used. It can be used in either “get” or “post”.

What is the difference between a "POST" and a "GET"?

The get method gives you an encoded URL and shows your password and information in the URL. The post method is more secure and doesn't post your information in the URL.
For login, you will want to use the “post” method. You will use “post” if you are trying to change, add, update, or delete something. You will use the “get” method if you just want to read something. “Get” is used when you click on links.
